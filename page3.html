The Garw Valley

A snippet about the middle of three valleys of Bridgend

Just as Bridgend is betwixt the two largets cities in Wales - Cardiff and Swansea, so the Garw is nestled just north of Bridgend and the M4 motorway, the Garw Valley is a singular sting containing the principal settlements of Llangeinor, Pontyrhyl, Pontycymer and Bleangarw.

Some have said 'Little Austria' but the truth is it is a relatively unknown valley. Cwm Garw is a true 'cwm' being headed with a steep-sided cirque - evidence of past glaciation. The head is too steep for a through road so the Garw remains 'one road in and one road out'.

Despite the mass unemployment event that came with pit closures and it's isolated geography, the Garw is truly a beeautiful valley. It has an abundance of footpaths, a national cycle route, a substantial Parc area including lakes and mountain bike trails and of course the 'mountains' that surround the valley..  If you too like the great outdoors then this is a place worth a visit.
